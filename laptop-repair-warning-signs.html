5 Signs Your Laptop Needs Professional Repair

January 3, 2026 6 min read

Laptops rarely fail suddenly. Usually, they give warning signs — small symptoms that hint at larger problems developing beneath the surface. The difference between a simple fix and an expensive repair often comes down to how quickly you respond to these early warnings.

Here are the five signs we see most often at Dhanu Computers — and what they usually mean.

01

Unusual Heat or Fan Noise

Your laptop getting warm during heavy use is normal. What's not normal: getting hot during basic tasks, fans running at full speed constantly, or the bottom becoming too hot to touch.

What's likely happening: Dust accumulation blocking airflow, degraded thermal paste, or failing fan bearings. Without proper cooling, your processor throttles performance to avoid damage — making your laptop slower and shortening its lifespan.

Urgency: Moderate. Continued overheating can cause permanent damage to the motherboard. A professional cleaning and thermal paste replacement typically resolves this.

02

Battery Draining Quickly or Bulging

Batteries naturally degrade over time — that's chemistry. But if your battery suddenly drains much faster than before, or if you notice the laptop sitting unevenly (the battery may be swelling), act quickly.

What's likely happening: Degraded battery cells, software issues keeping components awake, or in worst cases, a swelling battery that poses safety risks.

Important: A swelling battery is a safety hazard. If you notice your laptop's touchpad is harder to click, or the laptop doesn't sit flat anymore, stop using it and get it checked immediately.

Urgency: High for swelling batteries (safety issue). Moderate for rapid drain (could indicate failing battery or other hardware drawing excess power).

03

Strange Clicking or Grinding Sounds

Modern laptops with SSDs should be nearly silent. If you hear clicking, grinding, or repetitive mechanical sounds, something's wrong.

What's likely happening: If you have a traditional hard drive (HDD), clicking often means the read/write head is struggling — a sign the drive is failing. Grinding from fans indicates worn bearings.

Urgency: High for clicking HDDs. This is often the sound of imminent drive failure. Back up your data immediately and get the drive checked.

04

Frequent Crashes or Blue Screens

Occasional crashes happen. But if your laptop is crashing regularly, showing blue screens (BSOD), or freezing during normal use, something needs attention.

What's likely happening: Could be software (driver conflicts, malware, corrupted system files) or hardware (failing RAM, overheating, dying storage). Diagnosis requires methodical testing.

Urgency: Moderate to high. Frequent crashes often precede complete failure. More importantly, they indicate your data may be at risk.

05

Display Issues

Flickering screens, lines across the display, dead pixels that spread, or the screen working only at certain angles — these are signs of display or connection problems.

What's likely happening: Failing display panel, loose or damaged display cable, or GPU issues. Some display problems worsen over time; others stay stable.

Urgency: Varies. Occasional flicker might be a loose cable (easy fix). Spreading dead pixels or lines often indicate panel failure (more involved repair).

What Should You Do?

If you're experiencing any of these signs:

  1. Back up your important data. Whatever is wrong, protecting your data should be the first step.
  2. Note the specifics. When does the problem occur? What were you doing? Does it happen every time or randomly? This helps diagnosis.
  3. Don't ignore it. Small problems become big problems. A fan cleaning today could prevent a motherboard replacement next month.
  4. Get a professional diagnosis. Many problems are simple and inexpensive to fix when caught early. We'll tell you honestly what's wrong and whether repair makes sense.

Our approach: At Dhanu Computers, we diagnose before we quote. We'll explain what's wrong, what it takes to fix it, and give you an honest assessment of whether repair makes sense for your specific situation.

Prevention Is Better Than Repair

Many laptop problems are preventable with basic maintenance:

  • Keep vents clear — don't use your laptop on soft surfaces that block airflow
  • Shut down occasionally instead of always using sleep mode
  • Keep liquids away from your laptop
  • Consider a professional cleaning every 12-18 months, especially in dusty environments

Taking care of your laptop isn't complicated. And when something does go wrong, addressing it early almost always saves money and frustration.
